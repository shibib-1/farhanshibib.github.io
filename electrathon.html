01 — Overview

Project Overview

ElectraThon is a multi-semester electric vehicle design competition run through the IEEE student chapter at the University of South Florida. The project spans full vehicle design — from battery architecture to real-time data logging — with student teams managing every subsystem end-to-end.

My focus is on the electrical systems: battery pack design, embedded control firmware, and the telemetry pipeline that lets us monitor vehicle health in real time during runs.

Active Development Multi-semester build — updated each term

04 — Notes

Lessons & Observations

Working on a real vehicle with real power systems is a fundamentally different experience than coursework labs. Every decision — a connector choice, a wire gauge, a fuse rating — has consequences that show up physically. The stakes make you careful in a way that simulated work doesn't.

CAN bus was new to me coming in. The protocol itself is elegant, but the devil is in the node addressing and timing. Getting the BMS and Raspberry Pi talking reliably required careful bit-timing configuration and a lot of scope time.

More detailed write-ups will be added here each semester as the project progresses.
